**Mode:** single_agent perspective challenge. No decision is being made here — this surfaces

## Hidden assumptions surfaced
1. *"Daily" cadence is the right resolution.* Assumes opportunity-relevant signal arrives daily.
   For most solo digital-product bets, the signal that changes a decision arrives weekly-to-monthly,
   not daily. Daily cadence can manufacture false urgency.
2. *Consuming signal = acting on signal.* A feed produces awareness, not decisions. Without a
   forcing step (signal → one opportunity angle → drop or pursue), it becomes read-only noise.
3. *More signal reduces overwhelm.* The stated Job #1 is anti-overwhelm. A daily inbound feed is in
   tension with that goal unless it is hard-capped (the roster caps Hikari at max 3 signals — that
   cap is the actual load-bearing design, not the feed itself).

## Counter-position
The feed is a **nice-to-have that becomes load-bearing only if** each item carries a concrete
"opportunity angle for the owner's product space" and is capped. An uncapped/un-angled feed is
time-cost with no decision yield → it is the nice-to-have that "กินเวลา" you suspected.

## Sharpened question to carry forward (if pursued)
"Over the last 30 days, how many feed items would have *changed* a build/kill/sequencing decision?"
If the honest answer is <1/week, daily is the wrong cadence and weekly is sufficient.
